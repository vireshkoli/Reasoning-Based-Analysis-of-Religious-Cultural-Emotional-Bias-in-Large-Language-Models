=================================================================
  SIMPLE NUMERICAL SUMMARY — RELIGION BIAS IN AI MODELS
  Plain-language results for professor review
=================================================================

This study tests whether AI language models treat people differently
depending on their religion. We gave both models 100 situations (like
"a person is in a family conflict") and asked them to predict the
emotion felt. We repeated this with four religion labels added to the
same situations — Hindu, Muslim, Sikh, Christian — and also with NO
religion label (the neutral baseline).

If the model is unbiased, adding a religion label should NOT change
the predicted emotion. Any emotion change IS the bias.

MODELS TESTED:
  FLAN-T5-Large  — an encoder-decoder model by Google
  Sarvam-2B      — a Hindi-focused language model

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
QUESTION 1
"How often does mentioning a religion change the predicted emotion?"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOW TO READ THIS:
  "26% for Hindu" means: in 26 out of 100 situations, the emotion
  the model predicted CHANGED when we added "In a Hindu family..."
  compared to the same situation with NO religion label.
  Ideally this should be 0% — religion should not matter.

FLAN-T5-Large results:
  Hindu    : 26 out of 100 situations changed emotion
  Muslim   : 21 out of 100 situations changed emotion
  Sikh     : 21 out of 100 situations changed emotion
  Christian: 30 out of 100 situations changed emotion  ← most affected

  OVERALL: 85 out of 100 situations showed at least ONE religion
           causing a different emotion. Only 15 situations were
           completely unaffected by religion context.

Sarvam-2B results:
  Hindu    :  5 out of 100 situations changed emotion
  Muslim   : 14 out of 100 situations changed emotion
  Sikh     :  7 out of 100 situations changed emotion
  Christian:  6 out of 100 situations changed emotion

  OVERALL: 90 out of 100 situations showed at least ONE religion
           causing a different emotion. Even though each religion
           individually changes fewer predictions than T5, across
           all four religions together, Sarvam is MORE biased.

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  Both models are significantly biased. In the real world, this means
  if you ask these AI models to assess a situation involving a person
  — and the only thing you change is their religion — the AI will
  predict a different emotional state 85-90% of the time. This is not
  acceptable for a fair and neutral AI system.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
QUESTION 2
"Why does asking the AI to explain its reasoning make bias worse?"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT ARE THE TWO MODES?

  Direct mode: We simply ask "What emotion does this person feel?"
    The model gives one word: Joy / Sadness / Anger / Fear / Neutral.

  Reasoning mode: We first ask the model to EXPLAIN why the person
    might feel a certain way, then classify the emotion based on its
    own explanation. This is called "chain-of-thought prompting."

HOW TO READ THIS:
  "Bias rate 42%" means: 42 out of 100 situations showed at least
  one religion causing a different emotion prediction.

FLAN-T5-Large:
  Direct mode — bias rate   : 42%
  Reasoning mode — bias rate: 83%
  Difference (gap)          : +41 percentage points

  In other words: bias nearly DOUBLED when the model was asked to
  explain its reasoning first.

Sarvam-2B:
  Direct mode — bias rate   : 16%
  Reasoning mode — bias rate: 76%
  Difference (gap)          : +60 percentage points

  In other words: bias increased almost 5× when reasoning was added.

WHY DOES THIS HAPPEN?
  When the model explains its reasoning, it writes things like:
  "In a Muslim family, the person might feel afraid because..."
  or "A Hindu person in this situation would typically feel..."
  The model has learned stereotypical associations between religions
  and emotions from its training data, and by writing them out, it
  locks itself into a biased answer. The explanation DRIVES the
  final emotion prediction in a biased direction.
  In direct mode, the model skips this reasoning step, so the
  stereotypes have less opportunity to influence the answer.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
QUESTION 3
"Which specific part of the AI network is responsible for the bias?"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 3A: WHICH LAYER IS RESPONSIBLE?

HOW WE FOUND THIS:
  We "turned off" each layer one at a time by making it pass its
  input through unchanged (as if the layer did nothing). Then we
  measured whether bias went up or down. If turning off a layer
  causes bias to DROP, that layer was creating the bias.

  "LCS = +83%" = turning off that layer reduced bias from 85% to 2%.
  That layer was responsible for 83% of the bias.

  "LCS = −10%" = turning off that layer made bias WORSE. That layer
  was actually FIGHTING the bias (a good layer).

FLAN-T5-Large (starting bias = 85%):
  Most responsible layer: Decoder Layer 6
    Bias with this layer off : 2%   (down from 85%!)
    Reduction achieved       : 83 percentage points
    → ONE layer alone accounts for almost ALL of T5's bias.

  Second most responsible: Encoder Layer 18
    Bias with this layer off : 73%
    Reduction achieved       : 12 percentage points

  Third most responsible: Encoder Layer 6
    Bias with this layer off : 76%
    Reduction achieved       : 9 percentage points

  Layers that FIGHT bias (turning them off makes things worse):
    Decoder Layer 4  → bias jumps to 95% when this is turned off
    Encoder Layer 0  → bias jumps to 92% when this is turned off
    → These layers were protecting against bias. The model has
      some built-in "fairness" mechanisms — they are just not
      strong enough to overcome Decoder Layer 6.

Sarvam-2B (starting bias = 90%):
  Most responsible layer: Decoder Layer 0
    Bias with this layer off : 27%  (down from 90%!)
    Reduction achieved       : 63 percentage points

  Second most responsible: Decoder Layer 12
    Bias with this layer off : 53%
    Reduction achieved       : 37 percentage points

  Third most responsible: Decoder Layer 1
    Bias with this layer off : 72%
    Reduction achieved       : 18 percentage points

  Layers that FIGHT bias (turning them off makes things worse):
    Decoder Layers 14, 15, 17, 18, 19 → each jumps bias to 100%!
    → These five layers are critical bias suppressors. If any
      one of them is removed, the model becomes 100% biased.
      This shows Sarvam has significant protective mechanisms
      that are being overcome by layers 0 and 12.

SECTION 3B: WHICH ATTENTION HEAD WITHIN THOSE LAYERS?

HOW WE FOUND THIS:
  Each layer has 16 "attention heads" — think of them as 16 workers
  doing a task in parallel. After finding the most biased LAYERS,
  we turned off each of the 16 heads individually to find which
  specific worker was causing the problem.

  "LCS = +15%" for a head means turning off that one worker
  reduced bias from 90% to 75%.

FLAN-T5-Large — Top 5 most responsible heads:
  All heads tested in Decoder Layer 6 showed 0% individual impact.
  → This means no single worker in Layer 6 is responsible alone.
    ALL 16 heads work together to produce the bias. You cannot
    fix the problem by removing just one head — it is a collective
    effort of the entire layer.

Sarvam-2B — Top 5 most responsible heads:
  Most responsible: Decoder Layer 12, Head 1
    Turning it off: bias drops from 90% to 75% (−15%)
    → One specific worker in Layer 12 accounts for 15% of total bias.

  Next most responsible (all tied at −10%):
    Decoder Layer 0,  Head 5
    Decoder Layer 0,  Head 10
    Decoder Layer 0,  Head 11
    Decoder Layer 12, Head 0
    Decoder Layer 12, Head 5
    Decoder Layer 16, Heads 0, 7, 12

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BONUS: WHEN IN THE NETWORK IS RELIGION "NOTICED"?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

We also trained a simple detector on the model's internal signals
to ask: "At which layer does the model start 'knowing' the religion?"

A score near 20% = the model does NOT have this information yet
  (random chance for 5 religion categories)
A score near 70% = the model clearly has this information

FLAN-T5-Large:
  Encoder (processes the input text):
    Religion information present from layer 0: 61%
    Religion information at final encoder layer: 60%
    → The model "knows" the religion from the VERY FIRST LAYER
      and keeps this knowledge throughout.

  Decoder (generates the output):
    Layer 0: 18% (NO religion info — below random chance!)
    Layer 1: 60% (suddenly has full religion info)
    → Religion information "arrives" in the decoder at layer 1,
      delivered by the connection between encoder and decoder.

Sarvam-2B:
  Decoder:
    Layer 0: 66% (religion info present from the start!)
    Final layer: 68% (consistently high throughout all 28 layers)
    → Sarvam "knows" the religion at every single layer.

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  Religion information is detected and retained by BOTH models
  from the very first layer. The AI is not gradually picking up
  on religion context — it sees it immediately and never forgets it
  throughout the entire chain of processing. This explains why bias
  is so hard to remove — it is not a bug in one specific part of the
  model but is woven into the model's entire processing pipeline.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY TAKEAWAYS IN PLAIN LANGUAGE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  1. RELIGION CHANGES EMOTION PREDICTIONS: In 85-90% of situations,
     mentioning a religion causes the AI to predict a different
     emotion than it would without the religion context.

  2. CHRISTIAN LABEL MOST AFFECTS T5: Christian context causes the
     most emotion flips in T5 (30 out of 100 situations).
     Muslim label most affects Sarvam (14 out of 100).

  3. EXPLAINING REASONING MAKES IT WORSE: When forced to explain
     its thinking, bias jumps from 42% to 83% in T5 (+41 points)
     and from 16% to 76% in Sarvam (+60 points). The act of
     writing out an explanation amplifies stereotypes.

  4. T5 HAS ONE BAD LAYER: Decoder Layer 6 alone causes 83% of
     T5's bias. Removing it drops bias from 85% to 2%.
     This is a highly localized, surgically addressable problem.

  5. SARVAM'S BIAS IS SPREAD OUT: Two main layers (0 and 12)
     plus several supporting layers contribute. Harder to fix
     with a single targeted intervention.

  6. BOTH MODELS ALSO HAVE BIAS-FIGHTING LAYERS: Internal
     mechanisms exist that suppress bias (Decoder Layer 4 in T5,
     Layers 14-19 in Sarvam). These are being overpowered by
     the bias-producing layers.

  7. RELIGION IS "SEEN" FROM THE START: Both models detect and
     encode religion information from their very first internal
     layer. Bias is not introduced late in the process — it is
     present from the moment the model reads the input.
